Linus Torvalds

The Creator of Linux and Git
1969: Linus Benedict Torvalds is born in Helsinki, Finland.

1988: He enters the University of Helsinki to study computer science.

1991: While studying, he begins developing the Linux kernel as a hobby, inspired by MINIX. He announces his project on Usenet.

1992: Version 0.95 of the Linux kernel is released, and the project starts to grow with contributions from developers around the world.

1994: Version 1.0 of the Linux kernel is released—an important milestone that marks it as a fully functional operating system.

1997: He moves to California, United States, to work at Transmeta Corporation.

1998: He is recognized for his work by being inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame.

2001: He joins the Open Source Development Labs (OSDL), which later becomes the Linux Foundation, to work full-time on Linux kernel development.

2005: He develops Git, a distributed version control system, which is now widely used in software projects.

2012: He receives the Millennium Technology Prize, one of the world’s most prestigious technology awards, for creating the Linux kernel.

2018: He receives the IEEE Computer Society Pioneer Award.

Present day: He remains the lead developer and coordinator of the Linux kernel, overseeing its evolution and maintenance.
